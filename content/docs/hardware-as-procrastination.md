There is a specific kind of shopping that presents itself as work. It has browser tabs full of gear comparisons, a spreadsheet, and a strong inner narrative about workflow. Last week it nearly got me again: a hardware sampler, secondhand, good price, and a fully-formed theory about how it would change the way I make tunes.

I have data on this theory. The Pacer earned its place; I use it every session and it forced me to write software, which is the opposite of procrastination. The synth I bought in Berlin, on the other hand, currently performs the role of a weight that keeps a MIDI cable from sliding off the desk. The difference was never the gear. The Pacer was bought to remove a friction I hit daily. The synth was bought to become a person I was not.

The test I use now: have I hit the limit of what I own this week, specifically, with a file to show for it? Not in principle. This week. The sampler failed the test. The tab is closed. The tune got written on the piano, which was, as usual, sitting right there.
