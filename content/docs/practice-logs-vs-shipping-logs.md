---
title: "Practice Logs vs Shipping Logs"
ref: "033"
date: 2026-03-08
kind: thought
---

Musicians keep practice logs. Forty minutes of scales, twenty of repertoire, ten of pretending to sight-read. I kept one for years, back when I was doing my piano grades, and it worked: the log measured input, because for a student, input is the product.

At some point I noticed I was still keeping input logs, just with different nouns. Hours on the prototype. Days of research. A week "getting the architecture right." All faithfully recorded, all measuring the same thing my practice log measured, except I was no longer a student and nobody was going to examine me on my scales.

So this site logs output only. A line means something shipped: code someone can run, a document someone can read, a tune that exists as a file rather than an intention. There is no line for effort, which means weeks of hard work sometimes produce nothing here, and the emptiness is the point. A practice log tells you whether you are improving. A shipping log tells you whether you are producing. Both are useful. Only one of them can be mistaken for the other for years at a time.
