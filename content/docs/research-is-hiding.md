---
title: "Research Is the Socially Acceptable Form of Hiding"
ref: "045"
date: 2026-06-11
kind: thought
---

Nobody has ever looked at a person reading papers and accused them of avoiding work. That is what makes it dangerous.

I spent most of last week reading about onset detection. Spectral flux methods, machine-learning approaches, a 2012 survey I can now summarise from memory. All of it genuinely relevant to Autosampler. None of it necessary, because the slicer I already had was good enough to ship, and I knew that before I opened the first paper.

Real research reduces uncertainty about a decision you are about to make. Hiding-research accumulates knowledge about a decision you are avoiding. They look identical from the outside and feel nearly identical from the inside; the difference only shows up in the diff, where one produces a change and the other produces a more informed version of the same hesitation.

The tell, for me, is the absence of a question. Real research starts with something specific I need to know and ends when I know it. Hiding starts with a topic and has no exit condition, which is precisely what makes it comfortable. A topic cannot be failed.

The survey was excellent, for the record. The slicer shipped unchanged.
