---
title: "A Demo Is Not a Product"
ref: "036"
date: 2026-04-02
kind: thought
---

A demo answers the question "can this be done." A product answers the question "will this be done again tomorrow, unattended, for a stranger, while I am asleep."

The gap between the two looks small from the demo side. The demo works; surely the rest is cleanup. From the product side the gap is visibly enormous, because the rest turns out to be: the input nobody sane would provide, the error message that has to be written for a person you will never meet, the payment, the refund, the email that gets sent when something breaks at 3 a.m. and the answer to who reads that email, which is me.

Autosampler has been a demo for three weeks now. It slices my stems beautifully. The temptation is to keep feeding it my stems, because my stems are the ones it was, in every sense, trained on. The work that remains is to make it survive contact with audio I did not choose, and that work is boring, unphotogenic and the entire difference between the two questions above.

Demos are for proving something to yourself. Products are for proving it to someone who owes you nothing.
