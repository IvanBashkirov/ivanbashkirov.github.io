title: "Why the Tunes Are in the Log"
ref: "039"
date: 2026-04-26
kind: thought
---

People ask why the tunes sit in the same log as the code. The honest answer is that I tried keeping two lists and one of them died. The music list was private, so it quietly became a wish list: titles of things I meant to record, keys of things I meant to learn. A wish list with timestamps is still a wish list.

One list forces one standard. A tune gets a line under the same rules as a release: it has to exist as an artefact, dated, done, playable by someone who is not me holding my breath. "Worked on a tune" does not qualify, the same way "worked on the parser" does not. If it is not worth a line, it is not finished; if it is not finished, it does not exist.

There is a second reason. Keeping them together stops me from ranking the two halves of my life against each other. In one list, Tune 06 sits between a bug fix and a tax registration, all of them equally shipped. The log does not know that one of these is supposed to be a hobby. I have decided not to tell it.
