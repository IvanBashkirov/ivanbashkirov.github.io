Irish tunes are learned by ear and played until they live in the hands — nobody transcribes first. I am trying to build software the same way: hands before theory.

At the session nobody hands you the dots. You catch the shape of the A part, lose it, catch it again, and some Tuesday the tune is simply there, arriving through the whistle without consulting you. The software equivalent is building the smallest playable thing before reading a word about how such things should be architected. The Kesh took a month of wrong notes in public. So did the slicer. Both work now, and neither was designed so much as repeated until true.
